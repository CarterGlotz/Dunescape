# GDD: SEASONAL CATASTROPHES — Complete 7-Season Arc
### Solara: Sunfall · VaultSpark Studios · March 2026

---

## THE SEASONAL ENGINE

Each season is a **narrative skin over a stable mechanical core**. The combat, skills, dungeon structure, and social systems never change. What changes is the *reason* the sun is dying, which enemies serve that reason, and what the Oracle says about it.

This design allows infinite content at near-zero development cost. A new season requires:
- 1 seasonal config file (enemies, boss, sky color, season name)
- 12 lore codex fragments (text only)
- 20 Oracle dialogue lines (text only)
- 1 enemy sprite theme (reskin of existing sprite atlas)
- 1 legacy relic design (cosmetic item)

**Total estimated dev time per season: 2–4 sessions.**

## SEASON 1: THE WANDERING COMET

**Duration target:** 6–8 weeks
**Sun dimming cause:** The Wandering Comet harvests solar energy as it passes
**Tone:** Wonder, awe, fear. The desert has never seen the sky move before.

### Narrative Summary
A comet from the outer dark is passing through the solar system. It feeds on solar radiation — a parasite the size of a mountain. Every night, the sky shows the comet arc growing closer and brighter. Ancient texts describe it: "the Devourer that returns every thousand years." The Sunkeepers know it must be stopped. The Eclipsers believe it is a messenger.

### Visual Theme
- Sky: comet arc visible at all times, growing larger as sun dims
- Enemy tinting: cyan-white glow on all seasonal variants
- Map: occasional impact craters as new dungeon room type
- Living Map: graves near craters glow faintly cyan

### Seasonal Enemies
| Enemy | Description | Special Mechanic |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| Comet-touched Raider | Normal enemy + cyan glow | Knockback on hit |
| Impact Golem | Formed from crater debris | Immune to ranged |
| Comet Shard | Flying projectile enemy | Splits on death |
| Harbinger (boss) | Herald of the comet | Meteor shower special attack |

### Lore Codex (12 fragments)
1. "The first astronomers called it The Wandering Eye. It blinked once per millennium."
2. "When the Comet last came, three civilizations burned their boats to fuel the sun. It was not enough."
3. "The comet does not feed on fire. It feeds on belief. Every soul that stops believing in the sun is a meal."
4. "I have watched the sky for forty years. The Eye has never been this close."
5. "The Eclipsers say the comet is a gift. They are correct. All gifts have a price."
6. "Wave 20 survivors reported seeing the comet face turn toward them. I did not believe them until I survived Wave 20."
7. "The Oracle told me my grave would be found before the comet left. She was not wrong."
8. "If you read this, the comet is closer than it was when I wrote it. It always is."
9. "The Sunkeepers performed the first Great Rite three days ago. The comet did not slow."
10. "I think the comet is not feeding on the sun. I think it is building something."
11. "The last fragment of this codex is not yet written. You will write it with your death."
12. "The comet passes. The sun returns. And the desert remembers everything." *(Unlocked at Sunfall Event)*

### Oracle Dialogue (Key Lines)
- Phase 1: "The Eye of the outer dark has found us. I can see it in your eyes too — you've looked up."
- Phase 2: "Three thousand years ago, the comet fed for forty days. The records say the sun recovered. The records do not say at what cost."
- Phase 3 Broadcast: "Citizens of Solara. The Wandering Comet has claimed its first harvest. I count the fallen. Twenty thousand. The Eye grows brighter."
- Phase 4: "The last civilization that faced this comet built a shrine so large it could be seen from space. It still stands. It did not save them. I tell you this not to discourage you. I tell you because the shrine *worked*."
- Phase 5: "This is what the end of a season feels like. I have forgotten what the sun looks like. Have you?"
- Sunfall: "The comet passes. Now is the time. Light the sun, or the comet will return and find nothing left to feed on."

### Legacy Relic: Comet Fragment
A shard of cyan-glowing crystal worn as an accessory. Provides +3% ranged damage permanently. Glows brighter when the player's lifetime death count is high. "It remembers how many times you fell."

## SEASON 2: THE VOID PLAGUE

**Duration target:** 5–7 weeks
**Sun dimming cause:** Dimensional parasites feeding on solar radiation from within the desert itself
**Tone:** Horror, corruption, paranoia. The enemy is invisible until it isn't.

### Narrative Summary
Something came through the dungeon walls. At first, players noticed enemies behaving oddly — moving wrong, attacking in the wrong order. Then the void rifts appeared: cracks in the dungeon floor that poured cold light. The plague spreads from rift to rift. The sun isn't being consumed from outside this time — it's rotting from within.

### Visual Theme
- Corrupted zones on world map (spreading purple staining)
- Void rifts in dungeons: visual crack effects, damage-over-time aura
- Enemies: void-tinted, partially transparent, move in non-Euclidean patterns
- Shrines: visually "defending" against void spread — their radius shows as protective glow

### Seasonal Enemies
| Enemy | Description | Special Mechanic |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| Void-Touched Raider | Corrupted normal enemy | Heals on player taking void damage |
| Rift Stalker | Emerges from void cracks | Teleports when below 30% HP |
| Plague Host | Infected large creature | Spreads void aura on death |
| Void Warden (boss) | Dimensional parasite given form | Splits into 3 at 50% HP |

### Key Design Innovation: Purification Zones
Players can "cleanse" a void rift by spending 20 Sunstones. This temporarily (5 minutes) eliminates the void aura in one dungeon room and restores 0.0005% sun brightness. This is the first mechanic where small player actions have direct, immediate impact on global sun brightness — distinct from the Sunkeeper Solar Rites (which require Wave 30 completion).

### Legacy Relic: Void Lens
A cracked crystal that shows "what lies beneath" any tile. Allows players to detect hidden void rifts before stepping on them. "It shows truth. Truth is often void."

## SEASON 3: THE ANCIENT MACHINE

**Duration target:** 6–8 weeks
**Sun dimming cause:** A pre-civilization solar harvester has been reactivated by unknown hands
**Tone:** Industrial, conspiratorial, majestic. Someone knows more than the players.

### Narrative Summary
Beneath the eastern desert, archaeologists (in-game NPCs) discovered a structure older than any recorded civilization. It is a machine. It runs on sunlight. Someone activated it. The Sunkeepers believe it was the Eclipsers. The Eclipsers believe it activated itself. The Oracle refuses to say who built it. The players never find out who activated it — not in Season 3.

### Visual Theme
- Industrial pipe and gear decorative elements in dungeon rooms
- Mechanical enemies: clockwork, steam-powered, brass and iron
- Sky: faint beam of light pointing toward the machine's location
- Map: "Machine District" zone with unique tileset

### Key Design Innovation: The Sabotage Network
The Machine can be sabotaged by completing a special "Machine Room" dungeon variant. Each successful sabotage reduces the machine's drain by 0.001% per hour. But the machine's owners (unnamed mechanical enemies) repair the damage over time. The game becomes a collective race: players sabotaging, enemies repairing.

### Legacy Relic: Gear Fragment
A brass gear that pulses with stolen solar light. Grants +5% crafting speed. "It was not built by human hands. It understands craft the way water understands being wet."

## SEASON 4: THE STAR-EATER

**Duration target:** 7–9 weeks (longest season — most lore depth)
**Sun dimming cause:** A cosmic entity approaching the sun, described only as "it" in all records
**Tone:** Lovecraftian horror. Incomprehensible scale. A new faction emerges.

### Narrative Summary
The Oracle goes silent for 48 hours at season start. When she returns, she speaks only seven words: "It has been here before. We lost." The entity has no name — it erases its own name from records. Players start calling it The Star-Eater. Some players, deep in the Void Plague's aftermath, begin to worship it (a hidden third faction: The Beloved).

### The Beloved: Hidden Third Faction
Players who choose The Beloved gain:
- +30% loot rate in The Eclipse phase
- Deaths cause 2× sun dimming (they hasten the Star-Eater's feast)
- Unique sanity mechanic: "attunement" meter fills over time, causing visual distortions
- Cannot perform Solar Rites or leave normal epitaphs — only Star-Eater scripture

The Beloved are discoverable but not advertised. Finding the hidden recruitment NPC is a community ARG moment.

### Key Design Innovation: Sanity Mechanic
The Star-Eater's proximity (as the season progresses) causes a sanity score to deplete. At low sanity:
- Item descriptions become wrong
- Enemy names change to unpronounceable glyphs
- Wave counter sometimes reads incorrectly
- The Oracle's voice distorts

This is intentional, mechanical, and toggleable (accessibility option: disable sanity effects).

### Legacy Relic: The Star-Eye
A glowing void-black gem with a pupil. Grants vision of enemies through walls within 5 tiles. "It watches you. You watch back. Neither of you blinks."

## SEASON 5: THE TWIN SUNS

**Duration target:** 4–6 weeks (shorter — intensity)
**Sun dimming cause:** A second sun is born, but it produces "false light" that drains the original
**Tone:** Disorientation, beauty, betrayal. Nothing can be trusted.

### Narrative Summary
A cosmic event births a second sun. For three days, the desert celebrates — two suns! Double light! Double warmth! Then the deaths begin. The second sun's light is wrong. Items that appear to be loot are traps. Healing nodes drain HP instead. The second sun is a mimic, feeding on the belief that it is real.

### Key Design Innovation: False Light Traps
10% of all loot nodes in any run are false-light traps: they appear identical to real loot but produce damage instead. The tell: slightly wrong color temperature (the false sun's light is 200K cooler than the real sun's). Players who survive multiple waves develop pattern recognition for the color difference. Discussing it on Discord becomes a community skill-share moment.

### Key Design Innovation: The Eclipse Cycle
Every 4 real hours, the two suns' positions create a forced eclipse that lasts 10 minutes. During this window, all sun phase mechanics jump to Phase 5 regardless of actual brightness. Healing is minimal. Enemies are at peak strength. Surviving an Eclipse Cycle mid-run is the hardest challenge in the game.

### Legacy Relic: False Eye
A gem that glows with slightly wrong-colored light. Passively reveals false-light traps within 3 tiles. "It takes a liar to spot one."

## SEASON 6: THE LAST SOLAR WIND

**Duration target:** 6–8 weeks
**Sun dimming cause:** The sun goes silent — no new energy is being generated, only fading warmth
**Tone:** Melancholy, acceptance, reflection. The most narrative-heavy season.

### Narrative Summary
There is no catastrophe this season. The sun is simply... tired. It produces no new energy. The warmth is the memory of warmth. Ghost NPCs appear in the world — not enemy ghosts, but echoes of players from past seasons. They walk their old routes. They can't speak. They carry remnants of past season relics.

### Key Design Innovation: Ghost NPCs
Ghost NPCs are generated from high-wave historical player data. A player who reached Wave 28 in Season 1 now walks their final route as a ghost in Season 6. Players can interact with them: seeing their Season 1 epitaph, their faction, their wave count. The ghosts are a living museum.

### Key Design Innovation: Memory Fragments
Instead of lore codex fragments, this season drops "memory fragments" — short prose pieces written from the perspective of dead players from past seasons (procedurally generated from actual player data). "Here is what Kira thought about, running Wave 14, in a world that was 68% bright."

### Legacy Relic: Solar Memory
A fading amber gem that grows dimmer each season you don't play. If you return after being absent for a season, it brightens again. "It remembers warmth. So will you."

## SEASON 7: THE UNRAVELING

**Duration target:** 3–5 weeks (short, intense finale of the first arc)
**Sun dimming cause:** The laws of reality near the sun are failing
**Tone:** Meta-horror. The game itself becomes unreliable.

### Narrative Summary
The Oracle stops speaking in complete sentences. Item descriptions have errors — sometimes deliberate, sometimes wrong in ways that help players. Dungeon rooms occasionally connect incorrectly. The wave counter glitches. This is not a bug — it is designed chaos, presented as narrative.

### Key Design Innovation: Controlled Jank as Story
A config flag `unravelingActive: true` enables the following:
- 5% chance any item description is swapped with another item's description
- 2% chance wave counter displays wave-1 or wave+1 instead of actual wave
- 1% chance a dungeon room loops (exits back to the same room)
- Oracle dialogue has deliberate [CORRUPTED] sections

Players cannot tell what is intentional and what is a bug. This is the point. Community analysis of "real bugs vs story bugs" drives viral discussion.

### End of Arc: The Convergence
Season 7's Sunfall Event is the arc finale. The community fights a manifestation of The Unraveling itself — a boss whose health bar displays incorrectly, whose attack patterns include "false attacks" that look like animations but deal no damage, and whose death trigger reveals a truth: all seven catastrophes were connected. The Oracle knew. She was watching to see if the community could survive not just the enemy — but uncertainty itself.

### Legacy Relic: The Last Truth
A perfectly clear crystal. No powers. No bonuses. The description reads: "You survived the Unraveling. Some things are exactly what they appear to be." This is the rarest relic — only awarded to players who survived all 7 seasons.

## SEASON DESIGN PRINCIPLES (FOR FUTURE SEASONS)

1. **Every season must have one "genius moment"** — something the community will screenshot and share. Season 1: the comet growing in the sky. Season 4: discovering The Beloved. Season 5: false light traps. Season 7: the game itself lying to you.

2. **Every season must have one emotional beat** — something that happens in the narrative that makes players feel something unexpected. Season 6's ghost NPCs of deceased players are that beat.

3. **No season should require new core systems** — always build on the existing engine. A new season is a config change plus text content plus a sprite sheet.

4. **The seasonal legacy relic must be worth wanting without being pay-to-win** — purely cosmetic with a small cosmetic bonus. The relic tells others: "I was there."

5. **The Oracle is the game's storyteller** — she gets new lines every season, and her arc spans all 7 seasons. She knows the truth. She reveals it slowly.
